If You Eat Carrots,
You'll Poop Carrots

Yes. We know.

It's a strange title for an email.

But you'll understand in a minute. This might trigger something inside you.

Many years ago, back in our student days, we discovered a video on YouTube. It was a quick 12-minute talk by a man we instantly connected with.

And do you know what phrase, out of all the powerful ones he said in those 12 minutes, stuck with us the most? So deeply that it's still engraved in our minds today.

Yes.

That one you're thinking of.

"If you eat carrots,
you'll poop carrots."

Simple and powerful. This phrase opened a door in our minds and motivated us to do things differently. It triggered the mental shift we needed. It made us understand something fundamental.

And the man continued, word for word:

"If you eat carrots, what will you poop? Carrots. If you want to poop onions, what do you have to do?"

Option A · Pray. Doesn't work.

Option B · Eat onions. It's almost certain you'll poop onions.

"Yet, most people keep doing the same thing and don't understand why they keep pooping the same thing."

Genius.

But this man wasn't the only one to share this message throughout history. Even Albert Einstein is credited with something similar:

"Insanity is doing the same thing over and over again and expecting different results."

We hope you're starting to grasp the idea we want to share with you in this email.

We want to revisit the message that resonated with us so long ago so you can understand that, even though it's hard, exhausting, and time-consuming (we know), if you want your dance to evolve, you have no choice but to do something different.

And not just that. If you want everything you're building, all the hours and money you invest in learning, to create solid growth instead of a house of cards that collapses at the first challenge, you have to install this mindset program inside your dance: the easiest path isn't always the best path.

That's just how it is. The trail is right there, but most people walk past it.

So, what does it mean to do something different?

It means approaching dance from a new perspective. A groundbreaking perspective, one that comes from deep understanding. Uncovering the code behind the simulation.

And the first signal that you're ready is this one:

Follow the white rabbit. The trail of information you've been missing. The answers nobody gave you.

Option A · Pray. Take the blue pill. Do nothing, change nothing, and hope results come from unconscious practice in socials filled with people of all skill levels and different goals, or from the random workshops you attend, which is basically the same as praying. You can do this forever, but nothing will change.

Option B · Take the red pill. Follow the rabbit to where the information lives. Understand the code from logic, something your body won't forget. Then dare to be different, dare to feel different. Control everything on the dance floor so you can stop pooping carrots.

That part is up to you. Only you can decide to do something different.
